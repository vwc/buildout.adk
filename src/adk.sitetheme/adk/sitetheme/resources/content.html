Integration Language Course "German as a second language" according to the Immigration Act (Zuwanderungsgesetz)

Basic courses (Basic level A1), Advanced classes level A2 and B1: 25 lessons per week

Who are the Integration courses meant for?

This course is meant for foreigners, and migrants who have permission to live in Germany for an extended period of time (at least 2 years).

What is an Integration Course?

All of our integration courses include: Intensive language instruction with a total of 600 classroom hours consisting of 45 minute lessons and an orientation course with a total of 60 lessons.

The language course is divided into a 300 hour basic course and a 300 hour intermediate course. Upon completion of this course particiants are qualified to take the German Cercification Test which is recognized throughout Germany as well as the rest of Europe as proof of having knowledge of the German language at the intermediate level.

The final examination consists of two tests:

  • the German language test for immigrants A2-B1 ( DTZ)
  • the orientation course test

Taking the final test is free of charge. If you pass the language test at level B1 and the orientation course test, then you have passed the final examination. You will receive the "Zertifikat DTZ Deutschtest für Zuwanderer". If you pass the language test only at level A2, you are allowed to attend another 300 lessons.
