German Language Programmes for school classes and groups

The best way to learn German is to be in Germany and learn it in an interesting and motivating environment. Therefore ADK offers pupils the opportunity to attend a language course with their classes and teachers.

This study trips are our special offer for classes from abroad (12-18 years old) with escorts or teachers uncluding morning lesson and an interesting program taken care of professionally by our pedagogic staff. Our extra activity program can be booked with or without whole-day trips.

We have many years of experience in organizing stays and excursions for school classes and other groups. A ‘live’ experience of the language, e.g. in small projects, (paper chase, interviews …) in an authentic environment as well as the realisation that you can communicate successfully in a foreign language, can reduce the intimidation of speaking and motivate – far beyond the stay in Germany.

When you suddenly use the things you have learned in everyday life and you are successful without the pressure of getting good grades, then it is motivating … In the lessons, games are played, the pupils leave the classroom for little projects (paper chase, interviews etc.) as well as listening to German pop music.

If you are interested – speak with our teachers about the lessons or sit in on classes. We are also happy to organise contacts with German schools for you.

Good to know

The lessons are tailored to the language skills of the group and we only speak German in class. Classes make significant progress in just a short time.

Augsburg

Ever since its founding by the Roman Emperor Augustus, Augsburg has been an important trading city. In the 14th century the rich and influential Fugger and Welser families turned Augsburg into a city of international importance, a city of emperors, the home and workplace of many famous artists, sculptors and composers. The face of Augsburg has been shaped by its 2000-year history in connection with the styles of all the major architectural periods.

Today Augsburg is, as the third largest city in Bavaria with 275 000 inhabitants, a university city, the seat of the Swabian government, an important economic location for many international companies as well as a city in which young people like to live.
